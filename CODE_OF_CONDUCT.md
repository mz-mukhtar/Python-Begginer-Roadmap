# Community Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, students, instructors, and contributors pledge to make participation in the **Python Beginner to Practical Developer Roadmap** a harassment-free, welcoming, and empowering experience for everyone, regardless of age, background, education level, programming experience, ethnicity, gender identity, nationality, or religion.

---

## 1. Core Community Principles

Our community promotes:
- **Respect**: Treating all learners and educators with dignity.
- **Inclusion**: Welcoming absolute beginners and experienced contributors alike.
- **Patience**: Recognizing that learning programming takes time and persistence.
- **Constructive Feedback**: Offering helpful suggestions rather than harsh criticism.
- **Beginner Support**: Creating a safe psychological space for asking foundational questions.
- **Collaboration**: Working together to build clean, educational software projects.
- **Privacy**: Safeguarding personal student records and individual information.

---

## 2. Expected Behavior

All community participants are expected to:
- Use welcoming, inclusive, and professional language
- Respect different learning speeds and starting levels
- Give constructive, actionable feedback during code reviews
- Help peers solve problems without insulting or shaming them
- Respect diverse backgrounds and viewpoints
- Protect personal privacy and educational sample data
- Focus feedback on the technical work, never on the person

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behaviors include:
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Public or private threats
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit consent
- Deliberate disruption of classroom discussions or issue threads
- Encouraging academic dishonesty or plagiarism
- Sharing private student credentials, passwords, or API keys
- Abusive or hostile communication

---

## 4. Educational Community Guidance

Because this is a beginner-focused educational community, participants must observe these specific classroom standards:
- **Never shame beginners**: No programming question is ever "too simple" or "obvious."
- **Never mock mistakes**: Syntax errors and tracebacks are natural steps in the learning process.
- **Do not copy another student's work**: Original coding builds real developer skills.
- **Do not submit unexplained work**: Ensure you can explain every core part of submitted code.
- **Do not pressure peers for private data**: Always use fictional sample data.
- **Guide rather than give answers**: When helping peers, give guiding hints rather than taking over their keyboard.

---

## 5. Enforcement

Repository maintainers and classroom instructors are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and taking appropriate action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:
- Request immediate correction of unprofessional communication
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and pull requests that violate this Code of Conduct
- Restrict or terminate participation for repeated or severe violations

### Reporting Concerns
If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report your concerns directly to the repository maintainers through the repository's official communication channels. All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with fairness and confidentiality.
